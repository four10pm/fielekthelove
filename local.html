Around Bear Mountain Inn

There's a lot to do around Bear Mountain Inn, so we hope you'll make a weekend out of it.

If you do stay for the weekend, please join us for brunch at Bear Mountain Inn on Sunday, September 11 at 11:00am.

Hiking

There is a lot of great hiking around Bear Mountain Inn!

We recommend the Bear Mountain Trail up to the Perkins Memorial Tower, but there are many other trails nearby.

Hiking around Bear Moutain

Boating

There are hourly paddle and row boat rentals on the lake next to Bear Mountain Inn from 11am-6pm on weekends (weather permitting.)

Guests under 16 must be accompanied by someone over 16.

Trailside Museums and Zoo

The four museums are open from 10:30am-4:00pm.

Trailside Museums and Zoo

Field Day

Just want to relax? There's a large field in front of the Bear Mountain Inn if you want to have a picnic, play frisbee, or just hang out.

This field is adjacent to the lake, and also houses the Merry-Go-Round pavilion!

Bear Mountain Spa

The Bear Mountain Spa is located within the Bear Mountain Inn, and includes services like massages, facials, hair, and nails.

Bear Mountain Spa

Storm King Art Center

Storm King Art Center is an open air museum that is a 22 minute drive from our venue.

Storm King is open from 10:00am-5:30pm.

Storm King Art Center

Woodbury Common Outlets

If you'd like to go shopping, the Woodbury Common Outlets are a 16 minute drive from our venue.

Woodbury Common Premium Outlets

Apple Picking

There are lots of options around Bear Mountain for apple picking, and September is a great month for it.

One of the most popular is Barton Orchards.
